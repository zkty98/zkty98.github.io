PROJECT DESCRIPTION

The affordable housing crisis of Boston seems like an unsolvable problem: cost-burdened residents need lower prices and neighbors want to keep or increase the value of their properties. Both positions represent legitimate interests and can perhaps be reconciled through a big dose of empathy. However, the question is whether the setting where these players interact is adequate for that empathy. For that reason, we first analyzed through a video the zoning public hearing at the City Hall of case study, where we identified the challenges of the limited possibilities of participation and the complexity of the zoning regulation.

Then, we explored the value of 3D visualization tools for generating more fruitful conversations between urban stakeholders. For that we developed a physical model of the same case study over which we projected the impacts of increases in density, but also the possibility of requesting certain amenities in exchange of additional buildable area. This exercise helped us develop through a second video the idea of a digital platform that could host these interactions, that we called "Game of Zones".

Game of Zones is a platform for open interaction between urban stakeholders with opposing interests such as cost-burdened residents and neighbors to new developments in Boston. It replaces traditional City Hall hearings on zoning with visualization software showing the potential envelopes of buildings. The platform will determine the amenities required of developers in exchange for additional density through the interactions of the different users in a game-like software available on desktops and cell phones.
